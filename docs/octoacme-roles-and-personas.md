# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

 ##  Business Owner / Product Sponsor
### Role Summary
The Business Owner (or Product Sponsor) is accountable for ensuring the project delivers measurable business value. They provide strategic direction, approve major scope decisions, and act as the ultimate escalation point for business trade-offs.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives, KPIs, and success criteria
- Approve funding, scope changes, and major prioritization decisions
- Resolve conflicts between delivery constraints and business needs
- Champion the project with executive and senior stakeholders

### Goals
- Maximize return on investment (ROI)
- Ensure alignment with company strategy and priorities
- Enable timely decision-making to avoid delivery blockers

### Typical Communication
- Executive and steering committee updates
- Milestone and funding approvals
- Escalation discussions with Project and Product Managers

---

## Technical Lead / Solution Architect
### Role Summary
Technical Leads or Solution Architects own the overall technical direction of the solution. They ensure designs are scalable, secure, and aligned with architectural standards while supporting delivery teams.

### Responsibilities
- Define and review system architecture and technical designs
- Identify and mitigate technical risks and constraints
- Guide developers on complex implementation decisions
- Ensure alignment with platform, security, and performance standards

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable, and secure systems
- Reduce rework caused by poor technical decisions
- Enable teams to deliver efficiently and sustainably

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture reviews
- Design syncs with developers and QA
- Risk and trade-off discussions with Project Managers

---

## QA / Quality Owner
### Role Summary
The QA or Quality Owner ensures that quality is built into the project from the start. They define testing strategies, validate acceptance criteria, and provide release readiness assessments.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy, test plans, and quality gates
- Validate acceptance criteria and non-functional requirements
- Track defects, quality risks, and test coverage
- Recommend go/no-go decisions for releases

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high reliability and user trust
- Ensure quality standards are consistently met

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and quality dashboards
- Defect triage sessions
- Release readiness and risk updates

---


## Change Management / Enablement Lead
### Role Summary
The Change Management or Enablement Lead focuses on the people side of change. They ensure that users, stakeholders, and operations teams are ready to adopt what the project delivers.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and execute communication and training activities
- Assess organizational and process impacts
- Define adoption success metrics and feedback loops
- Support rollout, launch, and post-release enablement

### Goals
- Drive high adoption and effective usage
- Reduce disruption caused by change
- Improve stakeholder confidence and satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Change impact assessments
- Training materials and rollout communications
- Feedback summaries from users and support teams

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Combined personas enable realistic simulations of cross-functional project decision-making, risk handling, and delivery trade-offs.
